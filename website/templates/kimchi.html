Classic Kimchi

I am not Korean so this is definitely not the best kimchi recipe you’ll ever see. But it’s fast and easy, so here it goes.

    Ingredients:
  • Head of savoy cabbage
  • Big daikon radish
  • 2 bunches of scallions
  • Some fish sauce
  • Korean red pepper flakes - very different from normal ones
    Special Kitchenware Needed:
  • Clean mason or canning jars
    Instructions:
  1. A lot of time you’ll see recipes that have you make a pasta and spread that paste on cabbage leaves. That’s too much effort. Let’s start by throughout washing our cabbage, drying it and chopping into about 1 inch strips. You can salt them when rinsing too to crisp them up or when drying to really draw moisture out of it.
  2. Chop radish into 2 inch long matchsticks
  3. Do the same to the scallions, keeping the whites.
  4. In a large mixing bowl, mix some fish sauce and red pepper flakes together (not really sure how much I normally do, kind of just eyeball it based on how much solids you have and viscosity of the sauce that forms.
  5. Throw your chopped veggies into the mix and coat very thoroughly.
  6. Can this into mason jars, packed as tightly as you can, the volume will reduce as the mixture ferments.
  7. Leave at room temp for a day or two and then for 2 weeks refrigerated.
  8. After that it is ready to eat! The more it ferments the more complex the flavor gets.
